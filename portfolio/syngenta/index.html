Problem.

The seed industry faces significant challenges due to outdated practices, impacting efficiency, genetic diversity, and adaptability to climate change demands.

Modernizing seed processes is crucial for enhancing innovation, sustainability, and competitiveness. This transformation aims to meet global agricultural needs by optimizing seed distribution and innovation across LAN, LAS, Brazil, EMEA, and North America. It addresses logistical challenges and adapts technologies to diverse market needs, ensuring resilience and efficiency amid evolving environmental conditions.

Key Learnings

Juruá Pescados design project highlights sustainable practices, community engagement, and innovation to strengthen Amazonian fisheries while fostering economic and environmental synergy.


  • Emphasis on involving local communities and indigenous knowledge in fishery operations to ensure cultural and ecological alignment​.
  • Advocating for sustainable fishing practices that minimize environmental impact, particularly in preserving Amazonian biodiversity​.
  • Implementation of technology to enhance fish processing, ensuring higher value-added products for domestic and export markets.​
  • Targeting both local and international markets, leveraging the unique value of Amazonian fish species like pirarucu​.
  • Ensuring product traceability and certifications to enhance consumer trust and align with ethical commercial standards.
  • Creating economic opportunities for small-scale fishers, ensuring equitable profit sharing and empowering marginalized groups​.
